## SLIDE 4 — What Is an A+ Setup?

**Heading:** The Anatomy of a High-Probability Trade

Present as a visual card grid — each card represents one of the 7 A+ criteria:

🎯 **1. Clear Directional Bias** — The broader market trend (SPX, QQQ) supports your trade direction. You're not fighting the tide.

📊 **2. Key Technical Level** — Price is at a significant support, resistance, VWAP, or moving average level — not floating in no-man's land.

📈 **3. Volume Confirmation** — Above-average volume validates the move. Thin volume = low conviction = skip.

⏱️ **4. Favorable Time of Day** — The first 30 minutes and last hour of the trading day offer the highest-probability moves. Avoid the midday chop (11:30 AM - 2:00 PM ET).

🔢 **5. Defined Risk-to-Reward** — Minimum 1:2 R:R. You know your stop-loss and target before entering. If you can't define both, it's not a setup.

🧠 **6. Clean Emotional State** — You are calm, focused, and following your plan. If you just took a loss, are distracted, or feel the urge to revenge-trade — walk away.

📋 **7. Passes Your Checklist** — All of the above must be true simultaneously. 6 out of 7 is not A+. It's a B trade. Snipers don't take B trades.

Add a callout:
> "An A+ setup is not a prediction. It's a confluence of conditions where probability is stacked in your favor."

## SLIDE 5 — The Pre-Trade Checklist

**Heading:** Your Gate: Nothing Gets Through Without All Green

Present this as a visual checklist with green checkmarks and red X marks, designed to feel like a cockpit pre-flight checklist:

**THE SNIPER PRE-TRADE CHECKLIST**

| # | Gate | Check | Description |
|---|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Market Trend | ☐ | Is SPX/QQQ trending in my trade direction (or neutral for range plays)? |
| 2 | Key Level | ☐ | Is price at a significant S/R, VWAP, or MA level? |
| 3 | Volume | ☐ | Is current volume above the 20-day average for this ticker? |
| 4 | Time of Day | ☐ | Am I in a high-probability window (first 30 min or last hour)? |
| 5 | R:R Defined | ☐ | Is my R:R at least 1:2 with stop-loss and target set? |
| 6 | Emotional State | ☐ | Am I calm, focused, and not revenge/FOMO trading? |
| 7 | Position Size | ☐ | Is my risk ≤ 2% of account and within daily loss limit? |

**Scoring Rule:**
- **7/7 green = A+ setup → Execute**
- **5-6 green = B setup → Skip (or paper trade only)**
- **< 5 green = No trade → Walk away**

Add a callout:
> "Print this. Tape it next to your screen. Check every box before every trade. No exceptions."

## SLIDE 6 — Watchlist Construction

**Heading:** Your Universe: 5-10 Symbols, Not 500

Present as a structured guide with visual tiers:

**Why a Focused Watchlist Matters:**
- You can't master 500 tickers. You can master 5-10.
- Familiarity breeds pattern recognition. You start to "feel" when your symbols are setting up.
- Reduces FOMO — if it's not on your list, it doesn't exist.

**The 3-Tier Watchlist Framework:**

**Tier 1 — Core (Always Watching):** 3-5 symbols
- High liquidity (>1M avg daily volume)
- Tight bid/ask spreads on options
- Familiar price behavior
- Examples: SPX, SPY, QQQ, AAPL, TSLA, NVDA, AMZN

**Tier 2 — Rotation (Sector/Theme):** 2-3 symbols
- Sector leaders currently in play
- Rotate weekly based on market narrative
- Examples: If tech is leading → MSFT, META. If energy → XLE, CVX.

**Tier 3 — Catalyst (Earnings/Events):** 1-2 symbols
- Only when a specific catalyst is approaching (earnings, FOMC, CPI)
- Removed after the event passes
- Higher risk, higher reward — only for experienced snipers

**Build Your Watchlist Now:**
Show a blank template card with 10 numbered rows: Symbol | Tier | Why It's Here | Key Levels

Add a callout:
> "If you can't explain in one sentence why a ticker is on your watchlist, it doesn't belong there."

## SLIDE 8 — Time-of-Day Edge

**Heading:** When You Trade Matters as Much as What You Trade

Present as a visual timeline of the trading day (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET):

**🟢 HIGH-PROBABILITY WINDOWS:**

**9:30 - 10:00 AM ET | "The Opening Drive"**
- Highest volume and volatility of the day
- Institutional orders flowing, gaps filling or extending
- Best for momentum plays and gap-and-go setups
- ⚠️ Warning: Also the highest-risk window. Size down until you're experienced.

**3:00 - 4:00 PM ET | "Power Hour"**
- Second-highest volume surge
- Institutions positioning for close
- Great for trend confirmation and closing-range plays
- Key window for SPX/0DTE trades

**🟡 MODERATE WINDOWS:**

**10:00 - 11:30 AM ET | "The Reversal Zone"**
- Opening moves often reverse or consolidate here
- Good for fade setups if you have the pattern recognition
- Requires patience — wait for confirmation

**🔴 AVOID:**

**11:30 AM - 2:00 PM ET | "The Chop Zone"**
- Low volume, directionless, spread-widening noise
- Most retail losses happen here because traders force setups that don't exist
- Sniper rule: If it's lunchtime, your screens should be off (or you're reviewing, not trading)

Add a callout:
> "Add Time of Day to your checklist. If it's 12:30 PM and you're about to enter a trade — stop. Wait for Power Hour."
