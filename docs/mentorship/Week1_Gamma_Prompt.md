## SLIDE 5 — The Math That Actually Matters

**Heading:** Win Rate Is a Lie (Without Context)

Present this as a visual math lesson with large, clear numbers:

**Scenario A — High Win Rate, Still Losing:**
- Win rate: 80%
- Average win: $50
- Average loss: $300
- Over 10 trades: (8 × $50) - (2 × $300) = $400 - $600 = **-$200**
- 80% win rate. Still lost money.

**Scenario B — Lower Win Rate, Profitable:**
- Win rate: 50%
- Average win: $400
- Average loss: $150
- Over 10 trades: (5 × $400) - (5 × $150) = $2,000 - $750 = **+$1,250**
- 50% win rate. Made money.

**The key formula — Expected Value (EV):**

Display this as a highlighted formula card:
> **EV = (Win Rate × Avg Win) - (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)**
> If EV is positive, your system makes money over time. If negative, you will lose no matter how often you win.

Add a note:
> "At Trade In The Money, our 81% win rate works because we pair high selectivity with disciplined risk-to-reward. Win rate alone means nothing."

## SLIDE 6 — Risk-to-Reward Deep Dive

**Heading:** The R:R Framework

**What is R:R?**
Risk-to-Reward ratio compares how much you risk on a trade vs. how much you stand to gain.

Show a visual scale or bar chart:

| R:R Ratio | You Risk | To Make | Verdict |
|-----------|----------|---------|---------|
| 1:1 | $100 | $100 | Break-even system — need >50% win rate |
| 1:2 | $100 | $200 | Profitable at 40%+ win rate |
| 1:3 | $100 | $300 | Profitable at 30%+ win rate |
| 1:0.5 | $200 | $100 | Need 70%+ win rate to survive |

Add tiered guidance cards:

**Beginner Traders:** Target minimum 1:2 R:R on every trade. You are learning, so your win rate will be lower. Make up for it by ensuring your winners are twice as big as your losers.

**Intermediate Traders:** Aim for 1:1.5 to 1:3 R:R. You can start being selective about which setups justify tighter risk based on your journal data.

**Advanced Traders:** You should know your historical R:R by setup type. Use your Trade Journal analytics to identify which patterns give you the best risk-adjusted returns and allocate more capital there.

## SLIDE 7 — Capital Preservation

**Heading:** Rule #1: Protect the Account

Present these as bold, numbered rules with explanations:

**1. You can't trade if you're blown up.**
A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain to recover. A 20% drawdown only requires a 25% gain. Small losses are recoverable. Big ones end careers.

Show a drawdown recovery table:

| Account Drawdown | Gain Needed to Recover |
|------------------|----------------------|
| 10% | 11.1% |
| 20% | 25.0% |
| 30% | 42.9% |
| 50% | 100.0% |
| 75% | 300.0% |

**2. The 1-2% Rule.**
Never risk more than 1-2% of your total account on a single trade. If your account is $10,000, your max risk per trade is $100-$200. This is position sizing, not optional.

**3. Set a Daily Loss Limit.**
Define the maximum you'll lose in a single day before you stop trading. Recommended: 3-5% of account. When you hit it, you're done for the day. No exceptions.

**4. Selectivity > Volume.**
Our 81% win rate doesn't come from a magic indicator. It comes from only taking trades that meet strict criteria. Fewer trades, higher quality, bigger edge.

## SLIDE 9 — Knowledge Test

**Heading:** Week 1 Knowledge Check

Create this as an interactive quiz format (use Gamma's quiz/accordion features if available):

**Question 1:**
A trader has a 75% win rate with an average win of $80 and an average loss of $350. Is this trader profitable?
- A) Yes — 75% is an excellent win rate ❌
- B) No — the losses are too large relative to the wins ✅
- C) Can't tell without more data ❌
*Explanation: EV = (0.75 × $80) - (0.25 × $350) = $60 - $87.50 = -$27.50 per trade. This trader loses money despite winning 3 out of 4 trades.*

**Question 2:**
You have a $25,000 account. Using the 2% rule, what is the maximum you should risk on any single trade?
- A) $2,500 ❌
- B) $500 ✅
- C) $1,250 ❌
- D) $250 ❌
*Explanation: 2% of $25,000 = $500. This is your maximum risk per trade, not your position size.*

**Question 3:**
Your account drops 30%. How much do you need to gain to get back to breakeven?
- A) 30% ❌
- B) 42.9% ✅
- C) 50% ❌
- D) 60% ❌
*Explanation: If you start with $10,000 and lose 30%, you have $7,000. To get back to $10,000, you need a $3,000 gain, which is 42.9% of $7,000.*

**Question 4:**
You took 5 trades today. 3 were planned setups. 1 was FOMO after seeing a stock spike. 1 was a revenge trade after your first loss. What percentage of your trades were emotional?
- A) 20% ❌
- B) 40% ✅
- C) 60% ❌
*Explanation: FOMO and revenge trades are both emotional. 2 out of 5 = 40%. The goal is to get this to 0%.*

**Question 5:**
Which matters more for long-term profitability?
- A) Having the highest possible win rate ❌
- B) Having a positive Expected Value (EV) ✅
- C) Taking the most trades possible ❌
- D) Never taking a loss ❌
*Explanation: A positive EV means your system makes money over time regardless of short-term variance. Win rate without favorable R:R is meaningless.*

**Bonus Question — Personal Reflection (no right/wrong):**
After reviewing your last 20 trades, what percentage were emotional, FOMO, or revenge trades? Write your honest number in your Trade Journal.
