01 — Overview
What You'll Do in the Trade Journal
Your Week 1 assignment requires four things. The Trade Journal handles all of them.

● Log Your Last 20 Trades

Enter each trade manually using the New Entry form. Record the symbol, direction, entry/exit prices, and P&L. The Quick Form takes under 30 seconds per trade.

● Categorize Each Trade

Use the Behavioral & Psychology section to tag whether each trade was Planned, Emotional, FOMO, or Revenge. Add your mood before and after.

● Calculate Your Real Numbers

The Analytics tab auto-calculates your win rate, total P&L, profit factor, average win, average loss, and expectancy from your entries.

● Set Your Risk Rules

Document your max risk per trade and daily loss limit in your Setup Notes, so it's recorded and you can reference it every trading day.

Pro tip: If you have a CSV export from your broker, you can use the Import button to bulk-upload trades from Interactive Brokers, Schwab, Robinhood, E*Trade, Fidelity, or Webull. Then go back and add the behavioral data to each entry.

05 — Categorize
Tag Every Trade Honestly
Use the Tags field and Behavioral section to classify each of your 20 trades into one of these four categories.
🎯

Planned

Identified on watchlist. Entry criteria met. Stop-loss set before entry. Followed rules.

💔

Emotional

Entered based on fear, anxiety, or excitement. No pre-defined setup. Gut feeling trade.

👀

FOMO

Chased a move already in progress. Entered late because you couldn't stand missing it.

🔥

Revenge

Entered immediately after a loss to recover. Sized up. Ignored your rules to "get it back."

07 — Calculate
Your Week 1 Numbers
The analytics dashboard gives you most of these automatically. Here's how to read them and what to bring to the group call.
Expected Value (EV) per Trade
EV = (Win Rate × Avg Win) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)

Shown as "Expectancy" in your Analytics dashboard

Risk-to-Reward Ratio
R:R = Average Win ÷ Average Loss

Calculate manually: sum your winning P&Ls ÷ count of winners, then sum losing P&Ls ÷ count of losers

Emotional Trade Percentage
Emotional % = (FOMO + Revenge + Emotional Trades) ÷ Total Trades × 100

Use the Tags filter to count each category

The numbers you need for the Week 1 call:

From Your Analytics Dashboard

Win Rate — shown on the summary stats cards

Total P&L — shown on the summary stats cards

Profit Factor — above 1 = profitable, below 1 = losing

Expectancy — your EV per trade, shown in advanced analytics

Max Drawdown — how deep your account dropped

Calculate Yourself

R:R Ratio — average win $ ÷ average loss $

Emotional % — count emotional/FOMO/revenge tags ÷ 20

Max Daily Loss — define as 3-5% of your account

Max Risk Per Trade — define as 1-2% of your account

Planned vs. Unplanned — which category won more?

08 — Checklist
Week 1 Completion Checklist
Check off each item as you complete it. Bring this checklist to the group call.
  • Log into the Trade Journal at tradeitm.com/members/journal
  • Click + New Entry and log all 20 of your most recent trades
  • For each trade, fill in: symbol, direction, entry price, exit price, P&L
  • Switch to Full Form and fill in Behavioral & Psychology for every trade
  • Tag each trade with one of: planned / emotional / fomo / revenge
  • Record mood before and after each trade
  • Rate your discipline (1-5) and whether you followed your plan for each trade
  • Click the Analytics tab and review your Win Rate, P&L, and Expectancy
  • Calculate your R:R ratio (avg win ÷ avg loss)
  • Calculate your emotional trade percentage
  • Define your max risk per trade (1-2% of account) and write it in setup notes
  • Define your max daily loss limit (3-5% of account) and write it in setup notes
  • Filter by "planned" tag and compare P&L against emotional/FOMO/revenge trades
  • Check the Bias Detection section for any flagged behavioral patterns
  • Prepare your numbers to share on the Week 1 group call
Remember: The point of this week isn't to feel good about your numbers. It's to see the truth. Every great trader started by confronting their weaknesses. Your journal is now your mirror — use it every single trading day from here forward.
